Who Am I?

Hiya, I'm Ben!
I'm A Senior At Seattle University With A Specialty In Low-Level Development. I Work Mainly In The C++ And Other C-Family Languages On Simulations And Algorithmic Development. I Really Enjoy Doing The Heavy Lifting Of Computer Science, Making My Projects With The Least Amount Of External Libraries As Possible (Of Course Within Reason [I Think I'd Die If I Didn't Use glm On Matrices]) As I Enjoy Understanding My Codebase In And Out. I Got Into Coding Quite Early In My Life After Realizing My Other Passion--Environmental Restoration--Was Being Taken Over With Technology. I Grew Up Doing Online School And It Allowed Me To Be Acquainted With Computers Very Early In My Life So Making The Jump In Profession Wasn't That Huge Of A Leap. I Finally Committed After Some On-And-Off Assignments During A Highschool AP Computer Science Class In Which I Initially Was One Of The Weakest Students In The Class. I Had A Night In Which I Decided To Clock-In As Hard As I Could To See If It Really Was For Me And This Caused Me To Boot Up StackOverflow And I Fell Into A Rabbit Hole. That Night I Stayed Up Till 5am On A School Night And It Made Me Realize My Drive To Learn And Get Into This Sphere Of Science. I've Been Doing Project Development For My Personal Projects For Around 7+ Years And In These Years It Has Allowed Me To Greatly Grow As A Person And A Developer. I've Tried To Always Challenge Myself And Learn New Aspects Of Computer Science Like Doing Simulations For Polymorphic Hierachical Development; Also Doing A Morse Code Interpretor To Understand Sound Drivers And How To Parse Audio Buffers. Throughout The Years I've Tried And Done A Lot And Will Continue To Strive To Become A Better Developer.
